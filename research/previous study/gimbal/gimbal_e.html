A new compact robot for manipulating forceps using friction wheel and Gimbals mechanism

Purpose

　In abdominal surgeries, minimally invasive surgery (MIS) such as laparoscopic cholecystectomy is widely performed. In MIS, surgeon cuts only 3-4 small incisions about 10mm on the abdominal wall. Through those incisions, laparoscope and forceps are inserted into abdominal cavity (Fig.1). Inside the cavity, the same procedure as laparotomy operation is performed. This technique is less invasive than laparotomy, so patients have merits in reduction of postoperative pain, discomfort, medication, and length of hospital stay. However, MIS has some technical difficulties. The forceps have only four Degrees of Freedom (DOF), so surgeons need skills and abilities (Fig.2). As an engineering solution, forceps manipulating robot systems are developed.

　However, those systems also have problems. Most of robot systems in clinical application are large. Some conventional operating rooms are too small to install the robot system. Robot has the danger of collision with surgeons, and occupies the operating space above the abdomen.The purpose of this study is to develop a compact robot for manipulating forceps in abdominal surgeries that does not occupy operating space above the abdomen.

Methods

　We set the following requirements for the forceps manipulator to be developed:

  1. The system should be attached to the bedside.
  2. At least, three sets of robot system can be used in the operating field.
  3. No extra adapter is required.
  4. The manipulator provides the following 4 DOFs (Fig.3).
    • Rotation around the shaft of forceps(1 DOF)
    • Back and forth movement of the forceps(1 DOF)
    • Orientation of the forceps (2DOF)

　We adopted "friction wheel mechanism"(Fig.4) and "gimbals mechanism"(Fig.5) to realize a robot manipulator satisfying the abovementioned requirements. Friction wheel mechanism realizes the rotation around the forceps and back and forth movement. Gimbals mechanism realizes the two DOF for determining the direction of forceps.

　The friction wheel mechanism consisted of 3 rollers. Those 3 rollers were placed around the forceps shaft and held the forceps stably. When the forceps rotate, a roller moves spirally on the surface of the shaft (Fig.4). We used two pairs of friction wheel and ultrasonic motor for the rotation around the forceps and back and forth movement.

　Gimbals mechanism can simplify the mechanism for determining the direction of forceps. Pitch and roll movements are driven with a geared DC motor by linkage mechanism partly. Linkage mechanism makes it easy to separate sterilized and non-sterilized parts.

Conclusion

　Friction wheels had the function of both holding and driving forceps, so we succeed in making the system compact. The size was 42mm diameter and 89mm long and 250g in weight. Gimbals mechanism realized the two DOF for determining the direction of the forceps. The size was 39mm, 54mm, 70mm and 330g in weight. This manipulator is enough small to use three sets without occupying the operating space above the abdomen (Fig.6).
