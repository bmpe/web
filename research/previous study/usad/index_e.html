Development of bending and grasping manipulator for multi degrees of freedom ultrasonically activated scalpel

　New surgical techniques is realized by integration of a small functional module on the tip of the forceps that has multi degrees of freedom (DOF) since it enables compex manipulation and treatment of limited workspace. We developed a new multi-DOFs ultrasonically activated scalpel (UAS) prototype. This prototype has two DOFs of bending and grasping realized by antagonistic wire mechanism. The bending angle of this manipulator ranges + /) 120[deg], and the operating angle of the grasping blade ranges 30[deg]. Grasping torque is sufficient to grasp tissue to coagulate. We confirmed that our prototype could incise and coagulate tissue effectively throogh in vivo experiment. This newly developed multi-DOFs UAS is a powerful equipment for minimally invasive laparoscopic surgery.

Purpose

　Today ultrasonically activated scalpel (UAS) is widely used in laparoscopic surgery. The currently available UASs have a linear shape. This sometimes makes surgeon to use the device from undesirable approach that might cause complications in surgery. Such surgery requires highly skilled techniques because most existing surgical instruments are linear shape, so the approaching to treatment area are often limited because of the trocar on the abdominal wall. Difficulties in instrument manipulation increase risk of undesirable injury of organs during surgical operation. UAS is one of the instruments that the surgeons request to add multi degrees of freedoms (DOF) of manipulation. We have been developing a small ultrasonic transducer to be mounted on the tip of instruments; it need not transfer vibration through any long straight metal rod. It has a grasping blade to pinch tissue and push the tissue to the ultrasonic vibration blade. It is 12[mm] in diameter and 58[mm] in length and its vibration frequency in operation is 55.5[kHz] and its amplitude is 80[um]; it is compact enough to be arranged on the tip of the instrument. It can generate satisfactory large power to incise tissue. In this study, we developed a hand-held instrument incorporating this small transducer, which has a bending mechanism and a driving mechanism for grasping blade of transducer module using linear slider.

Method

　The new hand-held instrument has one DOF of bending mechanism and hand-operated grasping mechanism (Fig. 1). Each mechanism is operated by hand. We tried to evaluate usability of multi DOFs UAS realized with the small transducer through simple a non motor-actuated mechanism. In order to drive these mechanisms, we employed the bending joint with antagonistic wire drive mechanism based on rolling contact. Antagonistic wire mechanism needs two wires to activate one joint; pull one wire to activate joint to one side, pull the other wire to the other side (Fig. 2). The advantage of this mechanism using rolling contact is that the wire paths do not change in length and phase if other wires are driven; wire paths are independent of each other, so surgeons do not have to make cooperated operation of two or more wire paths to operate one motion of this instrument. This prototype is operated by hand, so this advantage is effective in particular. The size of bending joint is 14[mm] in length, and enables large bending angle of + /)120[deg]. To make opening and closing motion of the grasping blade, the linear slider drives the shaft arranged in the back-end of the transducer. To keep high accuracy of motion of wire mechanism, the pre-tension of wire must be kept in adequate level. If the pre-tension becomes improper, the motions of this instrument derail the course of surgeon’s intension. But it often occurs in the case of wire mechanism, adjusting the pre-tension of wire without any tension adjusting mechanism is very difficult. This is because wire tensions usually can only be adjusted in the assembling process. In order to make it possible to adjust the wire-pretension after being assembled, we introduced wire tension adjuster mechanism to the wire holder (Fig. 2). This mechanism utilize feed screw mechanism, drives the drum on which the wire is fixed parallel to the wire axis to elongate the wire paths. This mechanism enables to apply pre-tension to both of the antagonistic wire at once.

Result

　We conducted an in vivo experiment to confirm the clinical feasibility of the bendingUASforceps using a pig simulating laparoscopic surgery by the lifting up method. The instrument was able to coagulate and incise tissue such as mesenteriumand enteric canal (Fig. 3). Comparing the working space inside the abdominal cavity, the transducer size was large (82 [mm]), so that arbitrary positioning was not always possible. Miniaturization of the linear slider mechanism would be effective for downsizing.

Fig.3 In vivo experiment

Conclusion

　We developed a bending and grasping manipulator for the small ultrasonic transducer, and confirmed that the multi DOFs UAS is feasible. Multi DOFs of the instrument enable surgeon to apply UAS to the surgical fields with more dexterous and safer approaches. This indicates that the improved usability of multi DOFs UAS compared with conventional UAS. For the future works, first, we are going to robotize it to be able to realize sophisticated motion. Secondly, we are going to add more degrees of freedom. Finally, we are going to measure the grasping force in real time, because it is understood that the grasping force is an important factor that controls the state of coagulation and incision. So real time force monitoring would enables to control or select the coagulation or incision state.
