Research on Minimally Invasive Epicardial Electrophysiological Mapping System

Overview

Goal of this study is to develop minimally invasive epicardial electrophysiological mapping system that can be integrated with surgical robot system in order to realize more precise diagnosis, therapy and evaluation.

Background

Heart repeatedly contract and relax, namely beats by conduction system, so that functions as a pump for circulation of the blood. Arrhythmia is a condition that heat beating out of normal cycle continues, is assumed to be one of reasons causing cerebral infarction or myocardial infarction. Atrial fibrillation shows abnormally excited regions or abnormal accessory pathway in atrium, that is one of fatal arrhythmia. As surgical procedures to atrial fibrillation, catheter ablation and maze procedure are performed in general. catheter ablation procedure is performed by applying ablation to abnormally excited regions or reentrant circuits based on a diagnosis with the help of endocardial electrophysiological map. CARTO system (Carto XP EP Navigation System; BiosenseWebster Inc., USA) and EnSite system (Ensite System; St. Jude Medical, Inc., USA) are representative endocardial electrophysiological mapping systems for catheter ablation. The maps are generated by locally measured endocardial electrograms and their position data using vascular catheter electrodes. The procedures, however, are lengthy and high X-ray exposure is required during the catheter manipulation. On the other hand, maze procedure is basically performed on predetermined regions considered to have a high probability of abnormal pathway of excitation propagation. In the case of minimally invasive maze procedure and robot assisted maze procedure, ablation is performed by a small ablation device inserted through several small incision windows. Epicardial ablation is considered to be safer than endocardial ablation. However, an epicardial electrophysiological mapping system applicable to minimally invasive maze procedure is not established yet, and targeted ablation is difficult. This problem can possibly damage the normal beating function of the heart. Consequently, minimally invasive epicardial electrophysiological mapping system is required for realizing more precise minimally invasive arrhythmia surgery.

Methods

Figure 1 shows system configuration. Optical markers are fixed to epicardium for tracking its movement. Local epicardial electrograms inside of optical markers are measured using small multi-channel electrode array. The scenes are taken by monocular endoscope. Position of optical markers and electrode array are tracked by image-based tracking algorithm. Global electrophysiological map can be generated by several local epicardial electrograms and their location data. [1] The electrograms recorded in different times are synchronized based on electrocardiogram. Finally, global electrophysiological map is superimposed on the image area inside of optical markers from current endoscopic stream. Registration of global electrophysiological map is simply realized.

Fig.1 System Configuration

We developed small multi-channel electrode array and optical markers. The electrode array can be inserted through a trocar of 10mm in diameter, and fixed by suction system as shown in Fig.2. Its specification is determined based on the speed of excitation propagation and the performance of electrocardiographic recording system. [2]

Fig.2 Small Multi-channel Electrode Array

The optical markers are developed by attaching four blue markers on a silicon sheet as shown in Fig.3. [3]

Fig.3 Optical Markers

We also developed image-based tracking algorithm for optical markers and electrode array on the epicardium. The algorithm is based on condensation algorithm. [3, 4] All tracking data recorded on endoscopic image plane coordinate system are transformed to those of optical marker based coordinate system for more efficient global mapping as shown Fig.4. The transformation parameters are acquired using two-dimensional projective transformation. Transformation error can be minimized by applying inverse transformation for superimposing global electrophysiological map on endoscopic stream.

Fig.4 Coordinate Transformation by Two-dimensional Projective Transformation

Performance Test

Most of image-based tracking method can be easily affected by specifications of camera and environmental conditions. Therefore, accurate evaluation will be impossible if we do not take various test conditions into account. However, we used two kinds of evaluations for tracking performance. One is computer simulation test considered to be no noise condition. The other is preliminary performance test using precise multi-axis stage done in the laboratory as shown in Fig.5. [4, 5]

Fig.5 Preliminary Performance Test Configuration

According to the result of computer simulation test, when the width of electrode array is over than 60 pixels, and its speed is over than 45 pixels/frame, the tracking error rose sharply. According to the result of preliminary performance test, RMS errors in position and orientation tracking accuracy were 0.64mm and 0.65°, respectively. The processing time in tracking algorithm was constant at 46ms per frame under Windows XP SP3, Intel Core 2 Duo 2.66GHz Processor and 5-channel color video capture board (PCI-5531; Interface co., Japan).

In vivo Experiment

We also performed an in vivo experiment as shown in Fig.6 using an anesthetized swine weighing about 45kg for evaluating the validity and usefulness of our method. In vivo experiment was approved by the University of Tokyo Committee on Animal Resources. Local epicardial electrograms for ventricular area were measured and recorded, while optical markers and electrode array were being tracked.

Fig.6 In vivo Experiment and Electrode Mapping Result

We also made a global electrode array for measuring global epicardial electrograms at one time to assess the validity of the small electrode array tracking algorithm and global epicardial electrophysiological mapping. To compare global electrophysiological map based on our method and global map using global electrode array like Fig.7, orientations of excitation propagation in two global maps are nearly same.

Fig.7 Global Electrophysiological Maps by Global and Small Multi-channel Electrode Arrays

Conclusions

We proposed new method of tracking epicardial movement and electrode array using endoscopic stream. Preliminary performance test and in vivo experiment shows the validity and usefulness of our method. Especially, electrode array can be tracked by only endoscopic stream without using conventional three dimensional tracking systems. Therefore, we can expect cost reduction and simple configuration of system. Finally, simple registration is also realized by integrating endoscopic stream and global epicardial electrophysiological map.
