Tool Tracking System based on Sensor Fusion of Image Marker and IMU for Laparoscopic Surgery

    Laparoscopic surgery is a kind of minimally invasive surgery, which has been known as an effective surgical treatment. In laparoscopic surgery, physicians perform an operation using a dedicated surgical instrument which is inserted through the small holes on the abdomen. Advantages of laparoscopic surgery are faster recovery, less physical burden, low risk of secondary infection and so on. Although there are many advantages for the patient, the burden on physicians is bigger than in open surgery because of the difficulty faced in understanding the operative field and not being able to carry out direct palpation.

    So, the role of ultrasound probe in laparoscopic surgery is important. It provides physicians with spatial information of the tumor and blood stream in the tumor periphery. In particular, intraoperative diagnosis using the ultrasound probe is essential for physicians in resection of liver which is an organ that bleeds easily. However in this system, the physician must check image of laparoscope and ultrasound device at the same time. In addition, we can only obtain two-dimensional images with the ultrasound probe for use in laparoscopic surgery, so physicians have to mentaly construct the three-dimensional information of the affected area from two-dimensional images. Therefore, we propose a new surgical navigation system (Fig.1.), which overlay two-dimensional tumor information obtained from the ultrasound probe repeatedly and presents the three-dimensional information of the tumor on the laparoscopic image.

Fig.1 The surgical navigation system we proposed.

    Typical surgical navigation systems use X-ray CT images or MR images obtained from preoperative diagnosis. So it is difficult for them to reflect the organ deformation during surgery. In contrast, our proposed system uses image information obtained from intraoperative diagnosis with ultrasound probe. As we can get real-time information, our system can account for the organ deformation during surgery.

    To realize this system, we need to track the ultrasound probe. Surgical tool tracking system is an essential part of surgical navigation system. In the surgical navigation system that is currently being studied, we usually use optical tracking system (OTS) and electromagnetic tracking system (ETS). OTS is used in a lot of surgical navigation system because it has high accuracy. However, this system cannot track target if there is an obstacle between the camera and optical marker. ETS can track target even if there is an obstacle. However, the accuracy of this system is deteriorated, due to the influence of magnetic material in the vicinity of the operative field. Above all, it is difficult to introduce these systems because measuring instruments required for these systems are huge and expensive. On the other hand, it is easy to introduce the tracking system using the image processing of laparoscope as tracking device because we use surgical instrument as a measuring device. The laparoscopic image processing (LIP) is vulnerable to occlusion similarly, as in the case of OTS. In addition, the glare of the light source can cause failure in marker detection and measuring error.

    From the above background, we have developed a sensor fusion tracking system with LIP and Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU), and made evaluation experiments of this system. The accuracy and stability of IMU is not affected by the external environment. Therefor if LIP cannot detect target, IMU performs data interpolation. IMU measures differential quantities such as angular velocity and acceleration resulting in accumulation of measurement errors.

Fig.2 System overview

    In the tracking system we proposed, a target marker of LIP and IMU are mounted on the ultrasound probe (Fig.2). IMU includes 3-axis gyroscope and 3-axis accelerometer. We can obtain the posture and the position of a target marker in the camera coordinate system, , and angle velocity and acceleration of IMU in the IMU coordinate system, . So when subjected to sensor fusion, the output of IMU must be integrated and coordinate transformation performed. In this study, we implemented a band-pass filter and the extended Kalman filter in this system in addition to performing sensor fusion.
